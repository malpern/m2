M2 Scheduler

Saturday Morning Workflow

How Matt fills next week's schedule in three waves, one Saturday at a time.

Overview

Generate. Reach Out. Confirm.

Every Saturday morning, Matt books next week's training sessions. The system proposes a schedule, sends texts in waves, and helps Matt handle the replies.
📋

Generate

Algorithm proposes sessions based on priority, preferences, and availability.

📱

Reach Out

SMS sent in three timed waves. Standing slots skip outreach entirely.

✅

Confirm

Replies are classified. Matt handles edge cases. Sessions lock in.

Before Saturday

Prep Work (Thursday–Friday)

Two optional steps Matt can do before Saturday morning to make the process smoother.
A
Check Availability
Matt
Go to Schedule → Availability. The grid shows Mon–Fri + Sun, with time slots 3pm–7pm. Toggle any slots where you're unavailable next week.
Default availability persists week to week. Use weekly overrides (amber ring) for one-time changes like a dentist appointment on Wednesday.
B
Adjust Priority Rankings
Matt
Go to Schedule → Priority. Three weighted factors determine who gets first pick of time slots:
College Commitment (weight 5) — College-bound athletes score highest
Grade Level (weight 3) — Seniors above freshmen
Effort & Attitude (weight 2) — Behavior score from 1–10

You can also drag to manually reorder. Pinned clients always keep their rank regardless of score.
The Main Event

Saturday Morning

The five steps Matt follows every Saturday, typically starting around 9am.
1
Generate the Week
System
Click "Generate Week" on the Schedule page. The system runs a three-pass algorithm:
Pass 1 — Standing Slots: Clients with recurring weekly sessions (e.g., "Mon 3pm, Wed 3pm") are auto-placed first. These are locked — no outreach needed.

Pass 2 — First Session: Every remaining active client gets one session, assigned in priority order. The system tries their preferred day and time first, then cascades to open slots (3pm → 5pm → 6pm).

Pass 3 — College-bound Extras: College-bound athletes with maxSessionsPerWeek ≥ 2 get a second session on a different day.

How time slots are chosen: Currently the system uses the static preferredDays and preferredTime stored on each client's profile. These were auto-detected from historical calendar patterns during import. The system does not yet look at what the client actually booked last week — this is a planned improvement.
2
Review & Adjust
Matt
All generated sessions appear as blue "Proposed" blocks on the calendar. Matt can:
Drag to move — Relocate a session to a different day/time
Click to cancel — Remove a session that doesn't work
Add Session — Manually add a one-off session

This is the last chance to adjust before texts go out. Proposed sessions won't appear on Google Calendar until confirmed.
3
Send Wave 1
Matt
Go to the Outreach page and click "Send Next Batch". The first 8 highest-priority clients receive a text:
"Hey [firstName], are you free [Day] at [Slot] for a session?"

The system sends via SMS (Twilio). Each message is logged with a timestamp. The outreach dashboard updates to show "Awaiting Reply" for each client.
4
Waves 2 & 3 Send Automatically
System
The remaining clients are texted in two more waves, timed automatically:
Wave 2 — 45 minutes after Wave 1 (next 8 clients)
Wave 3 — 2 hours after Wave 1 (everyone else)
Follow-up — 1 hour after sending, non-responders get a nudge
Expire — 3 hours after sending, unconfirmed slots are released
5
Handle Replies
Matt
As clients reply, the system classifies each response. Most are automatic, but some need Matt:
"Yes" / "Sounds good" → Auto-confirmed. Session turns green. ✅
"No" / "Can't make it" → Not cancelled automatically. The system offers alternative open times from the current calendar, or asks if the client can't meet this week at all. Only cancel if the client confirms they're skipping the week entirely. 🔄
"Can I do Thursday instead?" → Flagged as Reschedule. Matt decides. 🔄
"Maybe" / unclear → Flagged as Ambiguous. Matt decides. ❓

The "Needs Your Decision" section on the Outreach page shows reschedule, declined, and ambiguous items with action buttons.
⏱
Typical timeline: Matt clicks Generate Week, reviews the calendar, sends Wave 1 around 9am. By noon, most clients have replied and the week is mostly locked in. Stragglers get follow-ups automatically.

Session Lifecycle

How a Session Moves Through the System

From algorithm proposal to completed training.
Priority Algorithm

Who Gets First Pick

When two clients want the same time slot, the priority score determines who wins.
1
College-Bound Athletes
Worth 50 points by default (10 × weight of 5). The single biggest factor. They also get a second session per week if max allows it.
2
Grade Level
Seniors score highest (4 × 2 × weight 3 = 24 pts). Freshmen get 6 pts. Adults get 0. Designed to prioritize athletes with imminent college deadlines.
3
Effort & Attitude
Matt's 1–10 behavior score, multiplied by weight 2. A client scoring 10/10 gets 20 pts. Rewards athletes who show up and work hard.
4
Manual Override
Drag-to-reorder on the Priority page bypasses the algorithm entirely. Pinned clients always keep their rank. Use this for VIPs or special circumstances.
Standing Slots

Recurring Sessions That Skip Outreach

Some clients train the same day and time every week. These are auto-filled with no text needed.
📌
A standing slot like Mon 3pm, Wed 3pm means the client always trains Monday and Wednesday at 3pm. The scheduler fills these first (Pass 1), before any other clients are considered. No SMS is sent — it's already confirmed.

Set standing slots on each client's detail page. Format: Mon 3pm, Tue 5pm; Thu 3pm, or Monday 15:00.
Wave System

Why Three Waves?

Sending all texts at once would create chaos. Waves let Matt process replies before the next batch goes out.
Wave 1
8 clients — highest priority. Sent when Matt clicks "Send Next Batch." These are the most important sessions to lock in first.
Wave 2
Next 8 clients — auto-sends 45 minutes later. By now, some Wave 1 replies are in and Matt can handle conflicts.
Wave 3
Everyone else — auto-sends 2 hours after Wave 1. The schedule is mostly set; these fill remaining gaps.
Follow-up
After 1 hour — non-responders get a nudge. "Hey, just checking — are you still free for that session?"
Expire
After 3 hours — unconfirmed slots are released and can be reassigned. The client can still confirm later via text.
When Matt Needs to Act

What Requires Your Attention

Most of the process is automated. Here's what only Matt can do.
Reschedule Request
Client wants a different time. Matt checks the calendar for an open slot, moves the session, and confirms.
Ambiguous Reply
System couldn't classify the response. Matt reads the message and clicks Confirm or Decline.
Manual Adjustment
Drag sessions on the calendar to resolve conflicts. If a confirmed session moves, Matt is prompted to text the client.
Session Movement SMS
When moving or cancelling a confirmed session, a pre-drafted text is shown. Matt can edit before sending.
Client Intelligence

What You See on Each Client's Page

Every client detail page shows scheduling intelligence derived from their booking history.
1
Last Week
Shows exactly what this client booked last week — day, time, and what percentage of their all-time sessions use that slot. Times used less than 10% of the time are flagged as "Unusual" in amber, meaning last week was an outlier and not their normal pattern. This helps Matt decide whether to propose the same time again or revert to their typical slot.
2
Schedule Pattern Heatmap
A day × time grid showing every slot the client has used. Core hours (11am–6pm) always show; off-hour times only appear if this client has used them. Color intensity reflects both frequency and recency — sessions in the last 30 days count at full weight, older ones fade with a 60-day half-life.
3
Session History Calendar
A monthly calendar view showing the last 30 days of sessions. Session days are highlighted with colored dots (green = completed, red = cancelled). Gaps of 14+ days between sessions are flagged as breaks with an amber indicator. Click "View all" to expand the full history.
4
Inline Editing
Session rate, type, effort score, standing slot, max sessions per week, and notes are all editable in place — click any value to change it. On the client list, grade and college-bound status are also inline-editable.
